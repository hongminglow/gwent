# Oathbound Game Rules Handbook

This handbook explains how to play the current first-playable version of Oathbound.

## Objective

Win the match by winning two rounds before your opponent.

Each round compares the total strength on both sides of the board. The player with the higher total wins the round. A match ends as soon as one player has two round wins.

## Match Setup

1. Choose one faction before entering a match.
2. The opponent receives one random faction from the remaining faction pool.
3. Each player starts with a leader card, a shuffled deck, and an opening hand.
4. During Opening Redraw, each player may replace up to two cards once.
5. When both players finish redraw, round one begins.

## Turn Flow

Players alternate turns while a round is active.

On your turn, you can:

- Play one card.
- Use your leader ability, if it is still available.
- Pass the round.

Passing means you stop taking actions for the rest of the current round. If your opponent has not passed, they may keep playing cards. The round ends when both players have passed or when neither player can act.

## Board Rows

Each side has three rows:

- Close
- Ranged
- Siege

Unit cards can only be played to allowed rows. Agile cards may choose between Close and Ranged. Your round score is the sum of your Close, Ranged, and Siege rows.

After a non-final round, most board cards move to discard. Monster decks keep one random normal unit on the battlefield as carryover.

## Card Types

### Unit

Unit cards add strength to a row. They can be affected by weather, Commander's Horn, Tight Bond, and Morale Boost.

### Hero

Hero cards add strength but ignore weather and strength modifiers. Hero cards cannot be targeted by Decoy or Medic.

### Special

Special cards apply tactical effects instead of acting as normal strength units.

### Leader

Leader cards sit beside the board and can be used once per match.

## Scoring Rules

### Weather

Weather reduces eligible units in the affected row to strength 1.

- Biting Frost affects Close.
- Impenetrable Fog affects Ranged.
- Torrential Rain affects Siege.
- Clear Weather removes all weather effects.

### Commander's Horn

Commander's Horn doubles eligible unit strength in one row.

### Tight Bond

Tight Bond multiplies matching units by the number of matching copies in the same row.

Example: two matching 4-strength units become 8 each before other eligible modifiers are resolved.

### Morale Boost

Morale Boost gives +1 strength to other eligible units in the same row.

### Hero Immunity

Hero cards ignore weather, Horn, Tight Bond, and Morale Boost.

## Abilities

### Agile

Can be placed in either Close or Ranged.

### Spy

Plays onto the opponent's board, then draws two cards for the owner.

### Medic

Revives one eligible normal unit from your discard pile.

### Muster

Automatically pulls matching group cards from deck or hand onto the board.

### Scorch

Destroys every strongest non-hero unit on the battlefield when their final strength is above 0. Scorch can destroy your own cards too.

### Decoy

Swaps a Decoy from hand with one eligible non-hero unit on your side of the battlefield.

### Clear Weather

Removes all active weather effects.

## Faction Perks

| Faction | Perk | Play Style |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Northern Realms | Draw one extra card after winning a round. | Balanced military pressure, siege strength, Tight Bond, and spies. |
| Nilfgaardian Empire | Win rounds that end in a draw. | Control tools, spies, medics, high-value units, and tie pressure. |
| Scoia'tael | Controls first-turn advantage through flexible opening pressure. | Agile placement and tactical opening control. |
| Monsters | Keep one random normal unit after each non-final round. | Muster swarms and carryover pressure. |

## Practical Tips

- Do not spend every strong card in round one.
- Passing early can force the opponent to waste extra cards.
- Spies are valuable because they create card advantage.
- Weather is strongest against crowded rows.
- Scorch is powerful, but it checks both sides of the battlefield.
- Heroes are stable points because they ignore most modifiers.
- Try to win two rounds with the fewest cards possible.

## Controls

- Hover a card to inspect it.
- Click or drag a playable card, then choose a valid row.
- Use Finish Redraw during Opening Redraw.
- Use Pass when you want to stop playing cards for the current round.
- Use Leader once per match when the leader action is available.
- Use Rules to reopen the in-game handbook overlay.
